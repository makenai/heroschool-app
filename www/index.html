Homelessness Intervention

HERO SCHOOL® STRATEGIC HOMELESS INTERVENTIONS

Q: "What do you do when you encounter a homeless person on the street?"

HS: Much depends on the environment as well as the comfort level and character type of the interventionist.

Hero School Interventionists are trained in THREE PRIMARY APPROACHES:

1. PRO ACTIVE: Interventionist asks a strategic, open-ended, human-centered question.
Ex: "Man, how long have you been out here?"
This is the best approach for interrupting homeless patterns before they can ask you for something.
Asking if the homeless person needs something shifts one to the Parent or Pet Owner Role and only activates extrinsic motivation and must be avoided.

2. REACTIVE: The homeless person asks you for something from you first.
Ex: “Excuse me, can I get a few dollars for the bus?"
Waiting to be asked a question is the most ineffective approach since it a) puts the interventionist in a "yes/no" position, under the authority of the homeless person’s personal management. Answering “yes” reinforces their management system and “begging” - Habit 1 - while answering “no” runs the risk of an argument, accusations and attack. Here, it is best not to answer.

3. RESPONSIVE: A homeless person has come up to your car while you're in it.
Ex. (Tap on the window, tries to get your attention)
Ignore the tapping and make him or her wait, like you would a salesman or other interruption at work or while dining with your loved ones. The longer you make someone wait, the more activity in the prefrontal cortex. Once you ready - in your time and on your terms - use APPROACH 1.

Remember, the only chance a person has to break free from the 4 Habits of Homelessness outside of a Hero School Experience is a strategic intervention on your terms.
